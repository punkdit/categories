Using the list:

Submissions should be sent to
categories@mta.ca

Administrative items (subscriptions, address changes etc.) should be sent to
categories-request@mta.ca
Categories uses Majordomo mailing list software. To receive list email at the address from which you are sending a subscription request, simply write "subscribe" in the body of your message. If you wish to have categories mailings go to a different address, add that address to your message. For instance, you might put the line "subscribe jdoe@my-uni.org" in the mail message body. To terminate your subscription, send the message "unsubscribe". This will fail if your provider has changed the way your address is shown in your mail. To remove an address other than the one from which you are sending the request, give that address in the request: "unsubscribe jdoe@my-uni.org".

Please be aware that since the list is moderated, posting of submissions will normally take some time. If your submission has not been posted within 48 hours it may have been discarded for one of the reasons below, or simply not received. After that time has elapsed you may contact the moderator.

List Policy (Revised 2014-09-03):
The subject of this moderated mailing list is category theory. Items on another subject may be posted if judged by the moderator to be of interest to subscribers. Discussion of such posts is excluded. The list is to be used for concise discussion that is suitable for email distribution. Posts are not transmitted if, in the moderator's judgement, they are unsuitable. Postings to the categories list will be courteous and respectful. Expression of disagreement must be polite. A submission judged by the moderator to be offensive or defamatory will not be posted. Submissions judged to be related such material will not be posted.

The categories mailing list distributes only text messages. Do not send attachments, messages containing html or excessively long postings (longer than about 10K). The default setting for some email clients is to compose messages containing html. This setting is easy to change. Postings with attachments or with html will be discarded.
Help with avoiding html is here or here.

A subject-sorted list of postings June 1994-December 1999 is at www.mta.ca/~cat-dist/catlist/

The full archives of postings on categories until 2009 (when Outlook made this impossible) are at www.mta.ca/~cat-dist/archive/. The postings are filed in yearly folders called 2006, 2005 etc. Within those folders there are monthly files.
